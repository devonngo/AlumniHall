Interhall Sports


Titles

We are the reigning champions in bowling and table tennis, and two-time defending Interhall A Basketball champions (looking for a three-peat in basketball). In the 2011-12 season, we brought home the O'Leary Cup.

In the 2017-18 season, we finished third in the O'Leary Cup standings, so it is extremely possible for us to bring the Cup back to the DawgHouse for the first time since the 2011-2012 season.

Recognition

Many dawgs participate in athletic events outside of intramural sports. Every week, a "Dawgthlete" is recognized for their athletic feats. Anything athletic can be submitted for a chance to be recognized on the wall of Dawgthletes. Instances include dawgs participating in activities outside of intramural sports, such as Shane Wagy's stepdancing and Riley Chelsky's Boston Marathon.

With the exception of Tackle Football, you will need to bring your own equipment for the sports that require it (gloves, rackets, cleats, skates, etc.). All dawgs are encouraged to play as many sports as their hearts desire, regardless of skill level or experience.

However, there will be tryouts and potentially cuts for football and basketball due to roster size limitations. As far as getting involved, the RecSports commissioners and team captains will generally reach out through DawgMail and/or mass announcements to let people know when signups are coming up. All participants must officially signup through IMLeagues.com for each of the teams they play on.

This is an extremely frustrating and convoluted process but it is necessary to play so just ask an upperclassmen dawg who has done it before if you need any help (RAs are a good resource). Also, all team and player fees are covered by the dorm so you really have no excuse not to pour your blood, sweat, and tears into Dawg Athletics.

Other Intramural Sports

There are also intramural sports which RecSports offers that are not interhall, including: Badminton, Broomball, Curling, Flag Football, Kickball, Softball, Ultimate Disc, and Wiffleball as well as some non-interhall divisions of certain sports (Basketball, Soccer, and Volleyball).

These leagues do not contribute at all to our campaign to assert dominance over the rest of campus through athletic prowess, but they are a good way to get highly coveted Intramural Champion shirt and CoRec sports are a great way to meet potential Lady Dawgs. You must, however, pay your own fees for these sports which is another reason to just save it for interhall, i.e. when it actually matters.

Additional Information

Any questions regarding interhall sports or RecSports in general should be directed towards your RecSports commissioners Chris Garbasz (cgarbasz@nd.edu) and Liam Champion (lchampio@nd.edu).

Good websites for reference are the RecSports Website and IMLeagues.
